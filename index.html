Statistics on SA literacy

Basic literacy among children in South Africa has declined. A new report from the 2030 Reading Panel has found.
Most children leave grade one without knowing the alphabet, while 82% of grade 4 children cannot read for meaning.
Fewer primary school children can read for meaning now than before the COVID pandemic.
Meaning becomes familiar when the child can resonate with the environment. On a daily basis children are accustomed to certain jargon and lexicon based on familiarity.

Why we offer what we offer

As we transition towards a different epoch in South Africa , it becomes vital to teach young people about society and more importantly the legal pillars that protect and strengthen democracy.

We dare not fail the young because of the current epoch. Children must understand the significance of the democratic dispensation and its significance in shaping society. Why people vote, why people petition, how people must petition. What parliament and the Legislature mean.

The democracy pack is a series of books for children between 7 and 12 to teach about the basic concepts of South African democracy.

Sample Books

The books are based on two young characters Tammy and Themba (Both aged 10). The two characters are a part of a class taught by the brilliant teacher, Miss Khumalo.

The individual books pertain fundamental themes within democratic dispensation. The themes are:

Ubuntu

Democracy

The Constitution

The Economy

Patriotism

Project Pilot

The Fundanomics project team has identified the Gauteng province as a pilot area for distribution of the democracy pack.
The region for the launch will be the City of Ekurhuleni. The idea is not just to print and distribute books, but for Fundanomics to be an integral part of the learning process and to entice the end user- the learner to see the benefit of understanding their country and the institutions of democracy.
